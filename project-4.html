Project 4 - Mental Health Insights in Tech: A Data-Driven Analysis

Developed a secure network infrastructure with VLAN segmentation, allocating IPs through a DHCP server and controlling access via DHCP snooping. Added multiple security layers such as port security and storm control on switches to prevent network flooding and unauthorized device connections. Enhanced resilience with an EtherChannel setup for redundancy, ensuring consistent network performance even in failure scenarios.

Step 1: Establish Mission and Data Cleaning

After determining the mission to be: identify whether company size or awareness of benefits has a greater impact on employees' treatment-seeking behavior. Understanding this can help improve workplace policies and mental health support. The data had plenty of input errors and inconsistencies so using python I cleaned it up.

After that, I used Python to create a series of visualizations with the cleaned data as part of the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) phase. Here, I was probing for trends and correlations that could reveal significant insights into mental health patterns in the workplace. I used graphs such as bar charts to observe treatment-seeking behaviors across company sizes, scatter plots to assess benefits awareness levels and their influence on seeking treatment, and box plots to examine the variability in work interference levels by company size. These visualizations helped guide the focus of my analysis, revealing intriguing patterns that merit further investigation.

With my conclusions in place, I turned to creating a clean, straightforward HTML page to showcase my findings. I focused on ensuring that the narrative aligned with the project’s objectives, aiming to present insights with clarity. Rather than just following the data blindly, I took a thoughtful approach, crafting a story that resonated with the mission and purpose behind the analysis.

The conclusion: Our analysis has shown that while company size remains an influential factor in shaping mental health support frameworks, awareness of benefits proves to be a stronger determinant of employees’ willingness to seek treatment.

To build healthier work environments, businesses should emphasize clarity in policy communication, promote accessible mental health benefits, and encourage proactive support strategies.
